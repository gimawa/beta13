Dimension — 89 m2
Location — Santa Lucia 437, Valparaíso, Chile
Typology — Cultural
Elaboration — 7th Bachelor‘s Semester (2019)
Group Members — Janek Küttner, Johanna Rosenkranz

The Super Salon 36 is a community space for the neighborhood of ‘Cerro Larraín’. It is located in Valparaíso, Chile. During a process of research and participation on site, we formulated requirements and wishes which we integrated into the final design.
Buildings on the coast of Chile have to resist different influences. The probability of an earthquake is due to the continuing plate tectonics particularly high. It is necessary to consider various lighter earthquakes a year. Therefore, we planned with wood as the primary material, because it is light, elastic and able to accept the resulting forces. We chose to plan and dimension with Raulí, a sustainable and regional-growing wood.
Furthermore, it is possible that raging waters and channels form during strong rainfalls in the streets. Considering the possibility of rising water and the strong topography on site, we planed the building elevated and in different heights to enable a sustainable handling of the columns in height and diameter.
In order to protect the inner building from the strong solar radiation during the summer months, we covered the most strongly affected north facade with bamboo, the east and the west facade whereas are protected by a roof overhang. That enables the sunlight to warm and lighten the room in the moderate winter.

For the formulation of the wishes and social requirements, we worked in a repetitive proceed of ‘speaking, demonstrating and acting’. We interviewed people around the community park, organized an exposition to demonstrate
and talk about our analysis work and prepared workshops with children as a playful way to indicate their needs. Taking all the knowledge of these activities as a basis, we formulated the design concept.
The basic wish for the building was the possibility of engaging various activities. As a result, we planned a flexible and open structure. That space is the basis of the Super Salon and can be individually interpreted and appropriated by its users. Storage is surrounding that free space and meets different purposes based on desired uses: A kitchen, a library, and a depot for furnishings. In that way, the storages activate the central room in a different way depending on the door you open. Additionally, the form of the roofs creates different situations for that space and guarantees the well-being of an individual within any group size. These factors form a community center that is integrated into its environment and into the lives of its users.
