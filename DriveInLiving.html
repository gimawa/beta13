Dimension — 6673 m2
Location — Wollankstraße 56, 10178 Berlin, Germany
Typology — Housing / Traffic
Elaboration — 3rd Bachelor‘s Semester (2016)
Group Members — Johanna Rosenkranz, Mona Schmid, Jessica Voth

During the Analysis of the Soldiner District in Berlin Gesundbrunnen, especially the ambivalence between the heavily trafficked Wollankstreet and the green Panke Area stood out. In between these axes tensions an urban space which is defined by lifeless streets with rows of parked cars and a wide array of parking lots and blocks. Thereupon, the car as an individual transport, but also it‘s a presence in the urban space was questioned. A car is used one hour a day on average and occupies a relatively large space. It is assumed that one shared car can replace 8 to 20 individual cars in urban transport. In the context of social housing, the idea of car and bike sharing was applied to all fields of living.

Considering the large space consumption of a car, the building is placed on a consisting parking block not far from the Wollankstreet. The existing column grid was adopted and elongated apartments with an east-west alignment were incorporated in that grid. An external gallery functions as the access to the apartments and forms larger community balconies depending on viewing directions. While the ground floor retains the function of a parking block for the neighbors, the residents can use the shared cars which are located on the first floor. Nevertheless, these cars are usable by the public.

Due to that concept, it is possible to park the same amount of cars in a row on a smaller space which provides extra capacity for a washing room for the inhabitants and a cafe for the public. Furthermore, the concept of a static car station within a building can provide electricity to the growing e-car industry without additional space consumption. In a future scenario, it is also intended to convert the parking spots on the ground floor to a car sharing space.
