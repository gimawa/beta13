On the area of the Kindl Brewery in Berlin Neukölln, sundry construction projects are planned and will be realized in the next few years. As a part of one site, we started our project and positioned it on top of the former, vacant basement of the brewery.
The Design-Built was developed within a semester‘s seminar with 36 students, who dealt with topics of sustainability, circularity, and recycling in the construction industry. The aim was to plan a flexible, temporary building and to realize it afterward.

A ‚space for everyone‘ which can be used by the social organizations around the area for events, meetings, and workshops with the neighbors had to be designed. The main focus while the planning process was on the decision of the material and construction to ensure the realizability with simple methods, the usage of recycled and sustainable materials and the free floorplan.
A rectangular construction with a terrace resulted. That terrace is covered by a wooden roof which is designed as a spatial frame structure with multiple overlaid beam layers. The roof membrane is divided into a surrounding transparent overhang and the inner insulated surface. The whole construction is carried by four columns which transfer the concentrated loads through a fixation to the basement ceiling. The insulated cardboard- and paper- manufactured walls tension the interior between these four columns. This interior space is surrounded by niches which provide different uses and enable various activities inside one room.
